Literature Survey

The Internet of Things (IoT) has become a significant concern in the field of cybersecurity due to its increasing adoption and the numerous vulnerabilities it poses. Various studies have been conducted to address these concerns, including those on reconnaissance attacks, DDoS attacks, and ransomware attacks. For instance, a study proposed a machine learning-based heuristic algorithm to combat SQL injection attacks, achieving a remarkable 93.8% accuracy rate. Another study presented a novel methodology for detecting unusual DDoS attacks using the Mirai dataset, with a combination of random forest and decision trees achieving an impressive accuracy rate of 99.7%.

Researchers have also explored the application of machine learning and deep learning techniques for IoT security. A study proposed a deep learning-based system for network intrusion detection on small routers at the IoT edge, achieving remarkable accuracy (99.991%) and low latency (less than 16ms). Another study designed a cloud-based IoT framework architecture, utilizing deep learning for real-time anomaly detection, yielding high accuracy (up to 99.99%). Additionally, a study reviewed various machine learning models for intrusion detection in IoT networks, concluding that Random Forest exhibited superior performance, albeit with higher processing power requirements. These studies demonstrate the potential of machine learning and deep learning in addressing the security concerns of IoT devices and networks.

Research Gap

The field of Network Intrusion Detection Systems (NIDS) on IoT edge devices faces several critical research gaps. Comprehensive studies comparing different ANN architectures for NIDS are lacking, as are standardized benchmarking frameworks to evaluate these architectures under varied conditions. There is limited research on deploying NIDS with ANNs on resource-constrained IoT edge devices and optimizing these models for efficiency without sacrificing detection accuracy. The unique challenges of adapting to dynamic and heterogeneous IoT environments, such as frequent device changes, are underexplored. Additionally, there is a scarcity of diverse datasets tailored for reconnaissance, DDoS, and injection attacks. Research is needed to develop lightweight deep learning models for resource-constrained devices and to enhance model robustness against evolving ransomware tactics through adversarial training.

Research Problem & Solution


Proposed Problem

This research aims to evaluate the impact of various ANN architectures on the performance of Network Intrusion Detection Systems (NIDS) on IoT edge devices. While the current study compares feedforward, shallow, deep, and hybrid ANNs, there is a need for a more comprehensive analysis covering a broader range of architectures. This expanded study will assess how different ANN models affect NIDS performance metrics, including accuracy, F1 score, sensitivity, specificity, and training time, to identify the most effective approaches for enhancing security in resource-constrained IoT environments.


Proposed Solution

To address the research gap and problem, we propose a comprehensive solution that leverages advanced techniques in artificial neural networks (ANNs) and edge computing to develop a robust and efficient Network Intrusion Detection System (NIDS) for IoT edge devices. The solution involves designing and implementing a range of ANN architectures, including feedforward, shallow, deep, and hybrid models, and training them on a diverse dataset of IoT network traffic, including reconnaissance, DDoS, injection, and ransomware attacks. The trained models are then optimized for deployment on resource-constrained IoT edge devices through pruning, quantization, and knowledge distillation, and are evaluated using a standardized benchmarking framework. Additionally, adversarial training techniques are employed to enhance model robustness against evolving ransomware tactics. The optimized models are then deployed on IoT edge devices and evaluated in real-world scenarios, considering factors such as device hardware and software limitations, network latency, and power consumption. The expected outcomes include a comprehensive analysis of ANN architectures for NIDS on IoT edge devices, optimized models that balance detection accuracy and computational efficiency, a standardized benchmarking framework, and a robust and efficient NIDS that detects a wide range of attacks in various IoT environments and scenarios.

Research Objectives

Evaluating ANN Architectures for NIDSe

This involves comparing the performance of different ANN architectures, including feedforward, shallow, deep, and hybrid models, in detecting various types of attacks, such as reconnaissance, DDoS, injection, and ransomware. The evaluation will focus on NIDS performance metrics, including accuracy, F1 score, sensitivity, specificity, and training time, to identify the most effective ANN architecture for NIDS on IoT edge devices.

Optimizing NIDS Performance

The research will investigate the effects of ANN architecture on NIDS performance in terms of detection accuracy, computational efficiency, and power consumption. This will involve evaluating the impact of ANN architecture on the trade-off between detection accuracy and computational efficiency, and identifying the most effective ANN approach for balancing these competing factors. The research will also explore the use of techniques such as pruning, quantization, and knowledge distillation to reduce the complexity of ANN models and optimize their performance on resource-constrained IoT edge devices.

Developing a Robust NIDS

This involves developing a dataset of IoT network traffic that includes a diverse range of attacks and normal traffic, and training and testing the NIDS on this dataset to evaluate its performance in detecting various types of attacks. The research will also investigate the effectiveness of the NIDS in detecting attacks in different IoT environments and scenarios, such as industrial control systems, smart homes, and healthcare systems.

Enhancing Security in Resource-Constrained Environments

This involves evaluating the performance of different ANN architectures and optimization techniques in terms of detection accuracy, computational efficiency, and power consumption, and identifying the most effective approach for balancing detection accuracy and computational efficiency. The research will also explore the use of adversarial training techniques to enhance the robustness of the NIDS against evolving ransomware tactics and other types of attacks. By achieving these objectives, this research aims to develop a robust and efficient NIDS that can effectively detect and prevent attacks in IoT environments and scenarios.

Methodology

Figure 2. High Level Architecture of the system.

The researchers developed a robust network intrusion detection system (IDS) to safeguard IoT edge devices against various cyber threats, including DDoS attacks, injection attempts, reconnaissance activities, and ransomware. To achieve this, they employed artificial neural networks (ANNs) and curated diverse datasets representative of different attack types, such as NF-BoT-IoT-v2, NF-ToN-IoT-v2, CICIoT2023, and Edge IIoT. They employed tailored data preprocessing and feature extraction techniques for each attack type, including label encoding, min-max scaling, mutual information gain, and Pearson correlation.

The researchers evaluated various ANN architectures, including feedforward, shallow, deep, and hybrid ANNs, and tuned hyperparameters to optimize performance for each attack type. They employed feature selection techniques, such as Extra Trees Classifier, to identify the most relevant features for each attack type. The IDS was trained using the curated datasets and evaluated using metrics such as accuracy, precision, recall, and F1-score. The conclusive model was stored as a TensorFlow model and executed on a Raspberry Pi 3 B+ (RPI) to demonstrate its effectiveness in real-world IoT edge scenarios. The researchers' methodology provides a foundation for further research, including exploration of alternative machine learning algorithms, hyperparameter optimization, and real-time performance evaluation on actual IoT devices.
